Sisig

A savory Filipino dish made with chopped pig's head, usually served sizzling with onions, chili, and a tangy dressing.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly, chopped
  • 1/2 lb pig's ears, chopped
  • 1/2 lb pig's face (optional), chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 fresh red chilies, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise (optional for creaminess)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 egg (optional, for topping)

Instructions

  1. Boil the chopped pig's ears and face (if using) in salted water until tender, about 1-1.5 hours. Once tender, remove from water and let it cool.
  2. While boiling, fry the pork belly in oil until golden and crispy. Once done, set it aside to cool and chop it into small pieces.
  3. Once the pig's ears and face have cooled, chop them into small pieces as well. Combine the pork belly, pig's ears, and pig's face (if using) in a bowl.
  4. In a hot pan, sauté the chopped onions and chilies until fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped pork mixture to the pan and sauté for 5-7 minutes until the pork is slightly crispy. Season with soy sauce, vinegar, and calamansi or lemon juice. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. If using, add mayonnaise to the pan and mix well for a creamier texture.
  7. Transfer the sisig to a sizzling plate and crack an egg on top (optional). Let the egg cook for a minute or two before serving.
  8. Serve with steamed rice and enjoy your sizzling sisig!
